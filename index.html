Climate Zones Lesson

What are the different climate zones of the US? What are their properties?

Total Points: 20

Major climate zones

Click on each climate zone below to learn its climate properties.

Tropical Rainy Climate

The "tropical rainy" segment includes Hawaii and the southernmost areas of Florida. This portion of the US is characterized by frequent rain (59 inches per year) and annual temperatures averaging 67.6°F.

Mixed Humid Climate

The "mixed humid" area of the US spans the Southeast region and is characterized by frequent rain (44 inches per year) and an average annual temperature of 53.65°F. The area is warm and humid, but unlike the tropical region, it may occasionally experience snow and freezing temperatures.

Arid Dry Climate

The "arid dry" region of the US is more commonly referred to as "desert". It has very little rainfall (less than 10 inches) and temperatures vary greatly between day and night. Mid-day temperatures average 65º while nighttime temperatures drop to an average of 43º.

Continental Humid Climate

The "continental humid" area of the US has many names, but it can most accurately be describes as having warm/hot summers and cool/cold winters. This area spans the largest of the US, covering the Mid-Atlantic, Northeast and northern mid-west. Temperatures and rainfall vary between seasons, but this region has an annual average temperature of 54.9°F and average rainfall of 45 inches.

Marine Coastal Climate

The "Marine Coastal" region runs along the Western coast of the US. Although being adjacent to the Continental Humid and Arid Dry climate zones, is characterized by much higher amounts of rainfall. Due to geographic features (particularly the Rocky Mountains) this area has an average annual rainfall of almost 70 inches per year. Annual average temperatures are 54º.

Subarctic Climate

The "subarctic" climate is the coldest, only found in Alaska and at very high elevations (such as the tips of the Rocky Mountains). This climate can be somewhat temperate in the summer, but on avereage is cold and dry. The annual average temperature is 38º while the average rainfall per year is 16.5 inches.
